Notes on Effective Interaction Using the Feshbach Projection Method in a Three-Body Model Space

Introduction

This discussion focuses on the effective interaction in a three-body model space using the Feshbach projection method. We consider the reaction of \(d + A\), where the system’s model space Hamiltonian is given by:

\[ H = T_{np} + T_{dA} + V_{np} + U_{pA} + U_{nA} \]

Unit Operator in the Model Space

The unit operator in this three-body model space can be expressed as:

\[ \mathbb{1} = \sum_{\alpha} \int R^{2} dR \left|\phi_{np}^{0} R \alpha\right\rangle \left\langle \phi_{np}^{0} R \alpha\right| + (2\pi)^{-3} \sum_{\alpha} \int R^{2} dR \int d\vec{k} \left|\phi_{np}^{\vec{k}(+)} R \alpha\right\rangle \left\langle \phi_{np}^{\vec{k}(+)} R \alpha\right| \]

According to PRC 108 034612(2023), the continuum states can be discretized, allowing the unit operator to be rewritten as:

\[ \mathbb{1} \approx \sum_{\alpha n} \int R^{2} dR \left|\phi_{np}^{n} R \alpha\right\rangle \left\langle \phi_{np}^{n} R \alpha\right| \]

For the \(d + A\) reaction, \(n = 0\) denotes the deuteron bound state, and \(n > 0\) indicates the discretized continuum states of the deuteron.

Projection Operators

We define the projection operators \(P\) and \(Q\) as follows:

\[ \begin{aligned} P &= \sum_{\alpha_0} \int R^{2} dR \left|\phi_{np}^{0} R \alpha_0\right\rangle \left\langle \phi_{np}^{0} R \alpha_0\right| \\ Q &= \sum_{n > 0} \sum_{\alpha} \int R^{2} dR \left|\phi_{np}^{n} R \alpha\right\rangle \left\langle \phi_{np}^{n} R \alpha\right| \end{aligned} \]

where \(\alpha_0\) is the channel index in which the projectile remains in its ground state. These operators have the properties \(P^2 = P\), \(Q^2 = Q\), \(PQ = 0\), and \(P + Q = 1\).

Feshbach Formalism

Using the Feshbach formalism, the three-body scattering state can be expressed as:

\[ \begin{aligned} \left\langle R\alpha | P\Psi^{(+)}\right\rangle &= \left\langle R{\alpha} \mid \varphi_{P}^{(+)}\right\rangle = \left\langle \phi_{np}^{0} R \alpha \mid \Psi^{(+)}\right\rangle \left|\phi_{np}^{0}\right\rangle \\ \left\langle R\alpha | Q\Psi^{(+)}\right\rangle &= \left\langle R{\alpha} \mid \varphi_{Q}^{(+)}\right\rangle = \sum_{n > 0} \left\langle \phi_{np}^{n} R \alpha \mid \Psi^{(+)}\right\rangle \left|\phi_{np}^{n}\right\rangle \end{aligned} \]

Schrödinger Equation for Scattering States

By projecting onto the ground state and bin states of the deuteron, the Schrödinger equation for the three-body scattering state is given by:

\[ \begin{aligned} (E - PHP) \left|\varphi_{P}^{(+)}\right\rangle - PHQ \left|\varphi_{Q}^{(+)}\right\rangle &= 0 \\ (E - QHQ) \left|\varphi_{Q}^{(+)}\right\rangle - QHP \left|\varphi_{P}^{(+)}\right\rangle &= 0 \end{aligned} \]

From the second equation, we obtain:

\[ \left|\varphi_{Q}^{(+)}\right\rangle = \frac{1}{E + i\varepsilon - QHQ} QHP \left|\varphi_{P}^{(+)}\right\rangle \]

Substituting this expression into the first equation, we derive the equation for the ground state component of the three-body scattering states:

\[ \begin{aligned} (E - PHP) \left|\varphi_{P}^{(+)}\right\rangle - PHQ \frac{1}{E + i\varepsilon - QHQ} QHP \left|\varphi_{P}^{(+)}\right\rangle = 0 \\ H_{\text{eff}} = PHP + PHQ \frac{1}{E + i\varepsilon - QHQ} QHP \end{aligned} \]

This formulation provides the effective Hamiltonian \(H_{\text{eff}}\) for the system, capturing the interactions within the model space.

Partial Wave Components

Returning to the first equation, we project the Schrödinger equation onto the deuteron ground state and express it in partial wave components:

\[ \begin{aligned} (E-P H P) |\varphi_P^{(+)}\rangle &= \Big( E - \sum_{\alpha_0 \alpha_0^{\prime}} \int R^2 R^{\prime 2} d R d R^{\prime} |\phi_{n p}^0 R \alpha_0\rangle \langle \phi_{n p}^{0} R \alpha_0 | H |\phi_{n p}^{0} R^{\prime} \alpha_0^{\prime}\rangle \langle \phi_{n p}^{0} R^{\prime} \alpha_0^{\prime} |\Big) \\ &\times \sum_{\alpha_0^{\prime \prime}} \int R^{\prime \prime2} d R^{\prime \prime} |\phi_{n p}^0 R'' \alpha_0''\rangle \langle \phi_{n p}^0 R'' \alpha_0'' | \Psi^{(+)}\rangle . \end{aligned} \]

Where

\[ \begin{aligned} \langle \phi_{n p}^0 R \alpha_0 | H | \phi_{n p}^0 R'\alpha_0^{\prime} \rangle &= \langle \phi_{n p}^0 R \alpha_0 | H_{n p} + T_{d A} + U_{n A} + U_{p A} | \phi_{n p}^0 R'\alpha_0^{\prime} \rangle \\ &= E_0 \frac{\delta(R-R^{\prime})}{R'^2} \delta_{\alpha_0 \alpha_0'} + T_{d A} \frac{\delta(R-R^{\prime})}{R'^2} \delta_{\alpha_0 \alpha_0^{\prime}} + U_{\alpha_0 0, {\alpha}_0^{\prime} 0} \frac{\delta(R-R^{\prime})}{R'^2} \end{aligned} \]

With \(U_{\alpha_0 0, {\alpha}_0^{\prime} 0}\frac{\delta(R-R^{\prime})}{R'^2}=\langle \phi_{n p} R \alpha_0 | U_{n A} + U_{p A} | \phi_{n p}^0 R^{\prime} \alpha_0^{\prime} \rangle\). In the partial wave basis, the equation becomes:

\[ \begin{aligned} \langle R \alpha_0 | (E - P H P) |\varphi_P^{(+)} \rangle &= \langle R \alpha_0 | \left( E - \sum_{\alpha_0'} \int R^2 \, dR \, |\phi_{n p}^0 R \alpha_0' \rangle (E_0 + T_{dA}) \langle \phi_{n p}^0 R \alpha_0' | \right. \\ & \quad \left. - \sum_{\alpha_0' \alpha_0''} \int R^2 \, dR \, |\phi_{n p}^0 R \alpha_0' \rangle U_{\alpha_0' 0, \alpha_0^{\prime\prime}0} \langle \phi_{n p}^0 R \alpha_0^{\prime\prime} | \right) \sum_{\alpha_0^{\prime \prime \prime}} \int R''^2 \, dR^{\prime \prime} \, |\phi_{n p}^0 R'' \alpha_0^{\prime \prime \prime} \rangle \langle \phi_{n p}^0 R^{\prime \prime} \alpha_0^{\prime \prime \prime} | \Psi^{(+)} \rangle \\ &= E\left\langle R{\alpha_0} \mid \varphi_{P}^{(+)}\right\rangle - (E_0+T_{dA})\left\langle R{\alpha_0} \mid \varphi_{P}^{(+)}\right\rangle- \sum_{\alpha_0^{\prime}} U_{\alpha_00, {\alpha}_0^{\prime}0} \left\langle R{\alpha_0'} \mid \varphi_{P}^{(+)}\right\rangle \end{aligned} \]

Interaction with Continuum

The interaction with the continuum is expressed as:

\[ \begin{aligned} P H Q |\varphi_{Q}^{(+)}\rangle &= \sum_{n > 0} \sum_{\alpha_0 \alpha^{\prime}} \int R^{2} \, dR \, R'^{2} \, dR^{\prime} |\phi_{n p}^{0} R \alpha_0\rangle \langle \phi_{n p}^{0} R \alpha_0 | H |\phi_{n p}^{n} R^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime}\rangle \langle \phi_{n p}^{n} R^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime} \mid \varphi_{Q}^{(+)}\rangle \end{aligned} \]

Where

\[ \begin{aligned} \langle \phi_{n p}^{0} R \alpha_0 | H |\phi_{n p}^{n} R^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime}\rangle &= \langle \phi_{n p}^{0} R \alpha_0 | U_{n A} + U_{p A} |\phi_{n p}^{n} R^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime}\rangle \\ &= U_{\alpha_0 0, \alpha^{\prime} n}(R) \frac{\delta(R - R^{\prime})}{R^{\prime 2}} \end{aligned} \]

In the partial wave basis:

\[ \begin{aligned} \langle R \alpha_0| P H Q |\varphi_{Q}^{(+)}\rangle &= \sum_{n>0} \sum_{\alpha^{\prime}} U_{\alpha_0 0, \alpha^{\prime} n} \langle \phi_{n p}^{n} R \alpha^{\prime} \mid \Psi^{(+)}\rangle |\phi_{n p}^{0}\rangle \end{aligned} \]

CDCC Equations

The three-body Schrödinger equation projected on the deuteron bound state can be rewritten as:

\[ \begin{align} \langle R \alpha_0| E-P H P\left|\varphi_P^{(+)}\right\rangle-\langle R \alpha_0| P H Q\left|\varphi_Q^{(+)}\right\rangle=0. \\ \Rightarrow \left(E-E_0- T_{dA}\right)\left\langle \phi_{n p}^0 R \alpha_0 \mid \Psi^{(+)}\right\rangle\left|\phi_{n p}^0\right\rangle-\sum_{\alpha_0^{\prime}} U_{\alpha_0 0, \alpha_0^{\prime} 0}\left\langle \phi_{n p}^0 R{\alpha_0^{\prime}} \mid \Psi^{(+)}\right\rangle\left|\phi_{n p}^0\right\rangle \\ -\sum_{\alpha^{\prime}} \sum_{n > 0} U_{\alpha_0 0, \alpha^{\prime} n}\left\langle \phi_{n p}^n R \alpha^{\prime} \mid \Psi^{(+)}\right\rangle\left|\phi_{n p}^0\right\rangle=0. \\ \Rightarrow \left(E-E_0- T_{dA}\right)\left\langle \phi_{n p}^0 R \alpha_0 \mid \Psi^{(+)}\right\rangle\left|\phi_{n p}^0\right\rangle -\sum_{\alpha^{\prime}} \sum_{n} U_{\alpha_0 0, \alpha^{\prime} n}\left\langle \phi_{n p}^n R \alpha^{\prime} \mid \Psi^{(+)}\right\rangle\left|\phi_{n p}^0\right\rangle=0. \end{align} \]

The above equation is the Coupled Discretized Continuum Channels (CDCC) equations, which describe the interaction of the deuteron with the target nucleus \(A\).

Effective interactions

In the partial wave basis, the effective Schrödinger equation for \(|\varphi_P^{(+)}\rangle\) becomes:

\[ \langle R \alpha_0 | (E-H_{\text{eff}}) |\varphi_P^{(+)}\rangle. \]

Here, the effective Hamiltonian \(H_{\text{eff}}\) is defined as:

\[ H_{\text{eff}} = P H P + P H Q \frac{1}{E+i \varepsilon - Q H Q} Q H P. \]

Substituting this into the equation, we have:

\[ \langle R \alpha_0 | E - PHP- P H Q \frac{1}{E + i \varepsilon - Q H Q} Q H P |\varphi_P^{(+)}\rangle \]

We have already computed the first part of this expression:

\[ \begin{aligned} &\langle R \alpha_0 | E - P H P |\varphi_P^{(+)}\rangle \\ &= E \langle R \alpha_0 | \varphi_P^{(+)}\rangle - (E_0 + T_{d A}^{\alpha_0}(R)) \langle R{\alpha_0} | \varphi_P^{(+)}\rangle - \sum_{\alpha_0^{\prime}} U_{\alpha_00, \alpha_0^{\prime}0} \langle R{\alpha_0^{\prime}} | \varphi_P^{(+)}\rangle. \end{aligned} \]

Next, we need to compute the term involving \(PHQ\) and \(QHP\):

\[ \langle R \alpha_0 | P H Q \frac{1}{E+i \varepsilon - Q H Q} Q H P |\varphi_P^{(+)}\rangle. \]

To evaluate this, we first consider the expression for \(Q H Q\):

\[ \begin{aligned} Q H Q &= \sum_{n>0} \sum_{n^{\prime}>0} \sum_\alpha \sum_{\alpha^{\prime}} \int R^2 dR R^{\prime 2} dR^{\prime} |\phi_{n p}^n R \alpha\rangle \langle\phi_{n p}^n R \alpha| H |\phi_{n p}^{n^{\prime}} R^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime}\rangle \langle\phi_{n p}^{n^{\prime}} R^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime}| \\ &= \sum_{n>0} \sum_{n^{\prime}>0} \sum_\alpha \sum_{\alpha^{\prime}} \int R^2 dR R^{\prime 2} dR^{\prime} |\phi_{n p}^n R \alpha\rangle \left[ (E_n + T^\alpha_{d A}(R)) \frac{\delta(R-R^{\prime})}{R'^2} \delta_{\alpha \alpha^{\prime}} \delta_{n n^{\prime}} + U_{\alpha n, \alpha^{\prime} n^{\prime}}(R) \frac{\delta(R-R^{\prime})}{R^{\prime 2}} \right] \langle\phi_{n p}^{n^{\prime}} R^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime}| \\ &= \sum_n \sum_\alpha \int R^2 dR |\phi_{n p}^n R\alpha\rangle (E_n + T_{d A}^\alpha(R)) \langle\phi_{n p}^n R \alpha| + \sum_{n>0} \sum_{n^{\prime}>0} \sum_\alpha \sum_{\alpha^{\prime}} \int R^2 dR |\phi_{n p}^n R\alpha\rangle U_{\alpha n, \alpha^{\prime} n^{\prime}} \langle\phi_{n p}^{n^{\prime}} R{\alpha^{\prime}}| \\ \end{aligned} \]

Similarly, we have the expressions for \(PHQ\) and \(QHP\): \[ \begin{align} PHQ &= \sum_{n>0} \sum_{\alpha_0 \alpha^{\prime}} \int R^2 dR R^{\prime 2} dR^{\prime} |\phi_{n p}^0 R\alpha_0\rangle \langle\phi_{n p}^0 R \alpha_0| H |\phi_{n p}^n R^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime}\rangle \langle\phi_{n p}^n R^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime}| \\ &= \sum_{n>0} \sum_{\alpha_0\alpha^{\prime}} \int R^2 dR R^{\prime 2} dR^{\prime} |\phi_{n p}^0 R \alpha_0\rangle U_{\alpha_0 0, \alpha^{\prime} n}(R) \frac{\delta(R-R^{\prime})}{R^{\prime 2}} \langle\phi_{n p}^n R^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime}| \\ &= \sum_{n>0} \sum_{\alpha_0 \alpha^{\prime}} \int R^2 dR |\phi_{n p}^0 R \alpha_0\rangle U_{\alpha_0 0, \alpha^{\prime} n}(R) \langle\phi_{n p}^n R \alpha^{\prime}|. \end{align} \]

\[ QHP=\sum_{n>0}\sum_{\alpha \alpha_0'} \int R^2 dR | \phi_{np}^n R\alpha \rangle U_{\alpha n,\alpha_0'0}(R) \langle \phi_{np}^0 R \alpha_0' | \]

Now, substituting these into our expression, we obtain: \[ \begin{aligned} & \langle R \alpha_0 | P H Q \frac{1}{E+i\varepsilon-Q H Q} Q H P |\varphi_P^{(+)}\rangle \\ & = \langle R \alpha_0 | \sum_{n>0} \sum_{n^{\prime}>0} \sum_{\alpha_0' \alpha^{\prime}} \sum_{\alpha^{\prime\prime} \alpha_0^{\prime\prime}} \int R^{\prime 2} dR^{\prime} R^{\prime\prime2} dR^{\prime\prime} |\phi_{n p}^0 R^{\prime\prime} \alpha'_0\rangle U_{\alpha_0' 0, \alpha^{\prime}n}(R^{\prime\prime}) \\ &\times \langle \phi_{np}^n R'' \alpha'|\frac{1}{E+i\epsilon-QHQ}|\phi_{np}^{n'}R'\alpha''\rangle U_{\alpha'' n', \alpha''_00}(R')\langle \phi_{np}^0 R' \alpha''_0 | \varphi_P^{(+)}\rangle\\ & = \sum_{n>0} \sum_{n^{\prime}>0} \sum_{\alpha^{\prime}} \sum_{\alpha_0''\alpha^{\prime\prime}} \int R^{\prime 2} dR^{\prime} U_{\alpha_0 0, \alpha^{\prime} n}(R) \langle \phi_{n p}^n R \alpha^{\prime} | \frac{1}{E+i\varepsilon-Q H Q} |\phi_{n p}^{n^{\prime}} R^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime\prime}\rangle U_{\alpha^{\prime\prime} n^{\prime}, \alpha_0''0}(R^{\prime}) \\ & \times \langle R^{\prime} \alpha_0'' | \varphi_P^{(+)}\rangle \end{aligned} \]

For the matrix element involving the inverse operator, we approximate as follows:

\[ \begin{aligned} & \langle \phi_{n p}^n R \alpha^{\prime} | \frac{1}{E+i\varepsilon-Q H Q} |\phi_{n p}^{n'} R^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime\prime}\rangle \\ & = \sum_{\alpha^{\prime\prime\prime}} \sum_{\alpha^{\prime\prime\prime\prime}} \int r^2 R^{\prime\prime\prime 2} dr dR^{\prime\prime\prime} r^{\prime 2} R^{\prime\prime\prime\prime 2} dr^{\prime} dR^{\prime\prime\prime\prime} \langle \phi_{n p}^n R \alpha^{\prime} | r R^{\prime\prime\prime} \alpha^{\prime\prime\prime}\rangle \langle r^{\prime\prime\prime} R^{\prime\prime\prime\prime} \alpha^{\prime\prime\prime\prime} | \phi_{n p}^{n'} R^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime\prime}\rangle \\ &\times \langle r R''' \alpha'''| \frac{1}{E+i\epsilon-\sum_{n''>0}\sum_\alpha \int \bar{R}^2 d\bar{R}| \phi_{np}^{n''} \bar{R} \alpha \rangle (E+T_{dA}^\alpha(\bar{R}))\langle \phi_{np}^{n''}\bar{R}\alpha|-\sum_{n''>0,n'''>0}\sum_{\alpha,\bar{\alpha}}\int \bar{R}d\bar{R} |\phi_{np}^{n''}\bar{R}\alpha\rangle U_{\alpha n'',\bar{\alpha}n'''}(\bar{R})\langle \phi_{np}^{n'''}\bar{R}\bar{\alpha}}|r' R'''' \alpha'''' \rangle \\ &=\int r^2r'^2 dr dr' \langle \phi_{np}^n | r\alpha'\rangle \langle r'\alpha'' | \phi_{np}^{n'}\rangle \frac{\delta(R-R')}{R'^2} \frac{1}{E+i\epsilon-\sum_{n''>0}\langle r\alpha'|\phi_{np}^{n''}\rangle(E_{n''}+T_{dA}^{\alpha'}(R))\langle \phi_{np}^{n''}|r' \alpha'' \rangle \delta_{\alpha' \alpha''}-\sum_{n''>0,n'''>0}\langle r\alpha' | \phi_{np}^{n''}\rangle U_{\alpha'n'',\alpha''n'''}(R)\langle \phi_{np}^{n'''}|r'\alpha''\rangle} \end{aligned} \]

Here, we treat \(T_{d A}^{\alpha\prime}(R)\) as a perturbation. Using the Born series expansion, we have:

\[ \frac{1}{E+i\varepsilon-\tilde{U}-\tilde{T}} = \frac{1}{E+i\varepsilon-\bar{U}} + \frac{1}{E+i\varepsilon-\bar{U}} \tilde{T} \frac{1}{E+i\varepsilon-\tilde{U}-\tilde{T}} \]

Where the operators \(\tilde{U}\) and \(\tilde{T}\) are defined as:

\[ \tilde{U} = \sum_{n^{\prime\prime}>0} \langle r{\alpha^{\prime}} | \phi_{n p}^{n^{\prime\prime}} \rangle E_n \langle \phi_{n p}^{n^{\prime\prime}} | r^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime\prime} \rangle \delta_{\alpha^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime\prime}} - \sum_{n^{\prime\prime}>0, n^{\prime\prime\prime}>0} \langle r{\alpha^{\prime}} | \phi_{n p}^{n^{\prime\prime}} \rangle U_{\alpha^{\prime} n^{\prime\prime}, \alpha^{\prime\prime} n^{\prime\prime\prime}} \langle \phi_{n p}^{n^{\prime\prime\prime}} | r^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime\prime} \rangle \]

\[ \tilde{T} = \sum_{n^{\prime\prime}>0} \langle r\alpha^{\prime} | \phi_{n p}^{n^{\prime\prime}} \rangle T_{d_A}^{\alpha\prime}(R) \langle \phi_{n p}^{n^{\prime\prime}} | r^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime\prime} \rangle \delta_{\alpha^{\prime} \alpha^{\prime\prime}} \]

And we take the first Born approximation:

\[ \frac{1}{E+i\varepsilon-\bar{U}-\tilde{T}} \approx \frac{1}{E+i\varepsilon-\bar{U}} \] Finally, we have:

\[ \begin{align} & \langle R \alpha_0 | PHQ\frac{1}{E+i\epsilon-QHQ}QHP|\varphi_P^{(+)} \rangle \\ & = \sum_{n>0,n'>0} \sum_{\alpha',\alpha''} \sum_{\alpha'_0} U_{\alpha_00,\alpha' n}(R) U_{\alpha''n',\alpha'_00}(R) \int r^2 r'^2 dr dr' \langle \phi_{np}^n |r\alpha' \rangle \langle r' \alpha'' | \phi_{np}^{n'} \rangle \frac{1}{E+i\epsilon-\tilde{U}} \langle R\alpha'_0 | \varphi_{P}^{(+)}\rangle \end{align} \]

Then the Schrödinger equation for the effective Hamiltonian becomes:

\[ \begin{aligned} & \langle R \alpha_0 | E-H_{\text{eff}} |\varphi_P^{(+)}\rangle = 0 \\ & \Rightarrow E \langle R \alpha_0 | \varphi_P^{(+)}\rangle - (E_0 + T_{d A}^{\alpha_0}(R)) \langle R \alpha_0 | \varphi_P^{(+)}\rangle - \sum_{\alpha_0^{\prime}} U_{\alpha_0, \alpha_0^{\prime}}(R) \langle R \alpha_0^{\prime} | \varphi_P^{(+)}\rangle \\ &-\sum_{n>0,n'>0} \sum_{\alpha',\alpha''}\sum_{\alpha_0'} U_{\alpha_00,\alpha' n}(R) U_{\alpha''n',\alpha'_00}(R) \int r^2 r'^2 dr dr' \langle \phi_{np}^n |r\alpha' \rangle \langle r' \alpha'' | \phi_{np}^{n'} \rangle \frac{1}{E+i\epsilon-\tilde{U}} \langle R\alpha'_0 | \varphi_{P}^{(+)}\rangle = 0. \end{aligned} \]
